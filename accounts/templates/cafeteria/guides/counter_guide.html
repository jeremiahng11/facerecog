Counter Staff Guide

Roles: Kitchen Counter / Cafe Bar Counter
Access: Counter Display, QR Scanner, Events
Last Updated: April 2026

1. Your Role

As counter staff, you manage the order display at your assigned counter (Local Kitchen, International Kitchen, or Cafe Bar). Your main tasks are:

  • Monitor incoming orders on the counter display
  • Prepare food items as orders arrive
  • Scan customer QR codes to confirm collection
  • Track no-show orders

2. Getting Started

  1. Log in with your Staff ID and Password
  2. You'll land on the Displays Hub
  3. Select your counter: Local Kitchen, International Kitchen, or Cafe Bar
  4. Keep this page open on your counter tablet — it auto-refreshes every 20 seconds

3. Counter Display Layout

The counter display has two areas:

AreaDescription
Left: OrdersGrid of order cards showing order number, customer name, item list, and status
Right: QR ScannerCamera view for scanning customer QR codes + HID scanner support

3.1 Order Cards

Each order card shows:

  • Order Number (large, e.g. H031, C019)
  • STAFF / PUBLIC badge
  • Time the order was placed
  • Status: Confirmed, Preparing, Ready
  • Customer name
  • Items list with quantities (only items for YOUR counter)
  • Mixed order indicator — if the order has items at another counter too

3.2 Tabs

  • Active — current orders waiting to be collected
  • No Show — orders ready for more than 20 minutes (not collected). Also shows auto-cancelled orders from today.

4. Scanning QR Codes

When a customer comes to collect their food:

  1. Ask them to show their QR code (on their phone or printed receipt)
  2. Point the tablet camera at the QR code, OR use the handheld HID scanner
  3. The system validates the QR and shows a result popup:
ResultWhat to Do
ValidHand over the food. Items are marked as collected.
Wrong CounterDirect the customer to the correct counter (e.g. "Please collect your cafe items at the Cafe Bar").
DuplicateThis QR was already scanned. The order has already been collected.
Not ReadyThe order hasn't been prepared yet. Ask the customer to wait.
InvalidThe QR code is not recognised. Ask the customer to show their order QR from the app.

5. Mixed Orders

Some orders contain items from both Kitchen and Cafe Bar (mixed orders). These show a "Mixed order" indicator on the card.

  • You only see items belonging to your counter
  • When you scan the QR, only your counter's items are marked as collected
  • The order card disappears from your display once your items are collected
  • The other counter still sees their items until collected separately

6. No-Show Orders

  • Orders not collected within 20 minutes (configurable by admin) move to the No Show tab
  • These are highlighted with a red border
  • At the daily cutoff time (set by admin), uncollected orders are automatically cancelled and credits refunded
  • You do not need to take any action — the system handles refunds automatically

7. Using a Handheld QR Scanner

If your counter has an external USB/Bluetooth barcode scanner (HID keyboard emulation):

  • The scanner types the QR data into a hidden input field
  • It auto-submits when it detects an Enter or Tab character
  • No need to tap anything — just scan and the popup appears
  • If using both camera and handheld, the handheld takes priority
Tip: If the camera stops working, try refreshing the page. The scanner auto-recovers focus every 800ms.

8. Events

  • Go to Events in the top navigation to see upcoming catering bookings
  • View event details: date, time, pax, menu selection
  • Use this to plan preparation for event catering
